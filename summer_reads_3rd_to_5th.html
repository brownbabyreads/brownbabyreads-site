Good Books to Read over the Summer

3rd to 5th Grade Books

Reading Level 3.0-3.9

Sassy: The Birthday Storm by Sharon Draper
While in Florida to celebrate her Grammy's birthday, nine-and-a-half-year-old Sassy worries that an approaching hurricane will ruin not only the party, but a nest of sea turtle eggs.
Reading Level: 3.6

The Real Slam Dunk by Charisse K. Richardson
Marcus studies hard to win the math contest prize and become the official greeter for Jason Carter, a professional basketball player.
Reading Level: 3.6

Trivia Queen, Third-Grade Supreme  by Derrick Barnes
When the Hope Road Academy holds its annual animal trivia contest, Ruby represents the third grade and competes against two of her older brothers.
Reading Level: 3.9

Secrets!: The Secret Life of David Mortimore Baxter by Karen Tayleur
David becomes famous for being good at keeping secrets. Big secrets. More and more people confide in him, until David's ready to explode.
Reading Level: 3

The Broken Bike Boy and the Queen of 33rd St. by Sharon Flake
Ten-year-old Queen, a spoiled and conceited African American girl who is disliked by most of her classmates, learns a lesson about friendship from an unlikely "knight in shining armor."
Reading Level: 3.5

Bird by Zetta Elliott
Young Mekhai, better known as Bird, loves to draw. With drawings, he can erase the things that don't turn out right. In real life, problems aren t so easily fixed.
Reading Level: 3.8

Willimena Rules: How to Face Up to the Class Bully Valerie Wilson Wesley
It 's the first day of school, and Willimena is scared! Tina, her know-it-all big sister, says Willimena should just smile and be nice. But a smile and "nice" won't get you very far if the class bullyalso known as Mean Irene--jumps in your face. And that's just what happened to  Willimena.
Reading Level: 3.2

Diary Of B.B. Bright, Possible Princess by Alice & Willams Randall
Held captive on an island, thirteen-year-old orphan Black Bee Bright must pass her Princess Test and complete a dangerous journey to the east side of the island where eight princesses help her discover what it truly means to be a princess.
Reading Level: 3.0

The Real Lucky Charm by Charisse K. Richardson
Thinking that luck, rather than hard work, has helped her succeed on the court and at school, ten-year-old Mia panics and turns to her twin brother for help when her lucky gold basketball goes missing from her charm bracelet.
Reading Level: 3.5

Amy Hodge Podge: Playing Games by Kim Wayans
Amy realizes her dream of playing sports when she joins the basketball league, and secret practices with Rusty improve her skills but her friends, believing she still plays badly, will not pass her the ball.
Reading Level: 3.8

Slam Dunk! By Sharon Robinson
At Harlem's Langston Hughes Middle School, eleven-year-old Elijah "Jumper" Breeze and his friends compete against Nia and her girlfriends on the basketball court, in a video dance tournament, and for a Student Council seat, and, meanwhile, several of the students face issues with their fathers.
Reading Level: 3.6

Liberty Porter: Cleared for Takeoff by Julia DeVillers
Now Liberty is boarding Air Force One and taking her message to the world: She’s going with her parents to Tbilisi, Georgia. When Liberty is around, you can only count on one thing: fun.
Reading Level: 3.7

Sugar Plum Ballerinas: Toeshoe Trouble by Whoopi Goldberg
Brenda tells her cousin Tiffany that she has a pair of ballet shoes autographed by the famous ballerina, Camilla Freeman, even though she doesn't and now she'll need the help of her Sugar Plum Sisters to get out of the mess!
Reading Level: 3.9

Miami Jackson Makes The Play by Patricia & Fred McKissack
Miami's enjoyment of summer baseball camp is threatened by the presence of his nemesis, Destinee Tate, but for once he finds himself on her side.
Reading Level: 3.4

Spike in Space by Malaika Rose Stanley
Spike's adopted family are all a little green in the face—that's because they're aliens! They're in a spaceship heading towards their planet, Aledela, and Spike is going with them. At first Spike is excited, but the planet is strange and he's picked on at school.
Reading Level: 3.0

Playing Forward by Eric Stevens
Hoping to become a forward for the Wildcats like his older brother, Isaac, who is quite short, learns that his coach would rather have him be a point guard.
Reading Level: 3.4

Running Rivals by Jake Maddox
Amy hurt her knee in a race, but now she is back on the team and will have to get her confidence back in order to compete.
Reading Level: 3.4

Halfway To Perfect: A Dyamonde Daniel Book by Nikki Grimes
Typically a normal eater (like Dyamonde), Damaris hardly has anything now. When Dyamonde confronts her about this, at first Damaris insists that nothing’s wrong, but Dyamonde thinks there’s more to the story
Reading Level: 3.8

Sugar Plum Ballerinas: Terrible Terrell by Whoopi Goldberg
Nine-year-old Terrel Liu tries to sabotage her father's new relationship with the aunt of her ballet class nemesis.
Reading Level: 3.9

Liberty Porter: New Girl in Town by Julia DeVillers
Nine-year-old Liberty Porter, daughter of the President of the United States, starts at a new school and tries to be an exemplary First Daughter.
Reading Level: 3.5
 

Sassy: The Dazzle Disaster Dinner Party by Sharon Draper
Sassy decides to host a fabulous dinner party, but things go terribly wrong the day of the party.
Reading Level: 3.4

Greg And The Cheat Sheets by Thalia Wiggins
Greg McNair works hard at school, at home, and in the community. He knew jumping into sixth grade would be tough, but he didn't think he'd be failing in the first week! Will the temptation to cheat be too much for Greg?
Reading Level: 3.6

Truth! David Mortimore Baxter Comes Clean  by Karen Tayleur
Caught "bending the truth" again, young David Mortimore Baxter decides to follow a strict diet of honesty which lands him in more trouble.
Reading Level: 3.5

The No. 1 Car Spotter and the Car Thieves by Atinuke
Oluwalase Babatunde Benson is the number 1 car spotter in the village - maybe even the world! Trouble is brewing in No. 1's village - cars are being stolen from outside Mama Coca-Cola's chop house.
Reading Level: 3.4

  

Reading Level: 4.0 - 4.9

A Friendship for Today by Patricia McKissack
In 1954, when desegregation comes to Kirkland, Missouri, ten-year-old Rosemary faces many changes and challenges at school and at home as her parents separate.
Reading Level: 4.1

Technical Foul by Rich Wallace
Jared, a high-scoring member of the Hudson City Middle School basketball team, gets angry when the point guard accuses him of being responsible for their string of losses, but finally realizes they can win only if he becomes a team player.
Reading Level: 4.1

Darnell Rock Reporting by Walter Dean Myers
A boy is given one last chance by the school principal to get his act together.
Reading Level: 4.3

Clubhouse Mysteries: Lost in the Tunnel of Time by Sharon Draper
Ziggy, Rashawn, Jerome, and Rico are thrilled to discover that their hometown was a stop on the Underground Railroad, and their friend Mr. Greene has given them an old map that shows a secret passage under their own school.
Reading Level: 4.4

Red Pyramid by Rick Riordan
Brilliant Egyptologist Dr. Julius Kane accidentally unleashes the Egyptian god Set, who banishes the doctor to oblivion and forces his two children to embark on a dangerous journey, bringing them closer to the truth about their family and its links to a secret order that has existed since the time of the pharoahs.
Reading Level: 4.5

Wedding Drama by Karen English
Ms. Shelby's third-grade girls are so excited about her upcoming wedding that they start a wedding planning competition, which drives a wedge between best friends Nikki and Deja, the two whose names were drawn to attend the event.
Reading Level: 4.5

One Crazy Summer by Rita Williams-Garcia
In 1968, Delphine and her two younger sisters travel to Oakland, California, to spend a month with the mother they barely know, and discover that she is resentful of the intrusion and wants them to attend a nearby Black Panther summer camp.
Reading Level: 4.6

President Of The Whole Fifth Grade
by Sherri Winston
To gain leadership skills needed to run a cupcake-baking empire when she grows up, Brianna runs for president of the fifth grade--expecting little competition--until a new girl enters the race.
Reading Level: 4.8

Out of My Mind by Sharon Draper
Considered by many to be mentally retarded, a brilliant, impatient fifth-grader with cerebral palsy discovers a technological device that will allow her to speak for the first time. Reading Level: 4.3

Brendan Buckley's Universe And Everything In It by Sundee Frazier
Brendan Buckley, a biracial ten-year-old, applies his scientific problem-solving ability and newfound interest in rocks and minerals to connect with his white grandfather, the president of Puyallup Rock Club, and to learn why he and Brendan's mother are estranged.
Reading Level: 4.0

Journey Through The Digestive System With Max Axiom, Super Scientist by Emily Sohn
In graphic novel format, follow the adventures of Max Axiom as he explains the science behind the human digestive system.
Reading Level: 4.8

Go Long! By Tiki & Ronde Barber
When Coach Spangler leaves at the start of their second year of junior high school, thirteen-year-old twins Tiki and Ronde wonder if his replacement, history teacher Mr. Wheeler, can coach the Eagles to another winning football season.
Reading Level: 4.9

Ellray Jakes is Magic! By Sally Warner
Third-grader EllRay, a "shrimpy, goof-up kid," discovers magic all around him when he and his schoolmates participate in the Oak Glen Primary School talent show and work together on a wedding shower gift for their teacher.
Reading Level: 4.0

 Nikki & Deja: The Newsy News Newsletter by Karen English
When Nikki and her best friend, Deja, start a newsletter about what is happening on their street and in their school, they focus more on writing exciting stories than on finding the truth.
Reading Level: 4.1

Shocking World Of Electricity With Max Axiom, Super Scientist by Liam O’Donnell
In graphic novel format, follow the adventures of Max Axiom as he explains the science behind electricity.
Reading Level: 4.9

The Mighty Miss Malone by Christopher Paul Curtis
Deza is the smartest girl in her class in Gary, Indiana, but the Great Depression challenges her skills as she and her family fight to survive.
Reading Level: 4.7

Red Zone by Tiki & Ronde Barber
Identical twins Ronde and Tiki's excitement over the approaching state championship football game turns to worry when there is a chicken pox outbreak at Hidden Valley Junior High.
Reading Level: 4.8

Nikki & Deja: Birthday Blues by Karen English
As her eighth birthday approaches, Deja's biggest concern is whether her father will attend her party, until her aunt is called away on business and a classmate schedules a "just because party" on the same afternoon.
Reading Level: 4.3

S.T.A.T.: Slam Dunk by Amar'e Stoudemire
When some kids get on eleven-year-old Amar'e Studemire for not being able to dunk, he sets a goal to make it happen, but when Amar'e's playing time is put on hold, he must face all of the other things he's been neglecting, in chapter-book format.
Reading Level: 4.4

Great Cake Mystery: Precious Ramotswe's Very First Case
By Alexander McCall Smith
Before becoming the first female private investigator in Botswana, eight-year-old Precious Ramotswe tracks down a thief who has been stealing her classmates' snacks.
Reading Level: 4.5

Celeste's Harlem Renaissance 
by Eleanor Tate
In 1921, thirteen-year-old Celeste leaves North Carolina to stay with her glamorous Aunt Valentina in Harlem, New York, where she discovers the vibrant Harlem Renaissance in full swing, even though her aunt's life is not exactly what she was led to believe.
Reading Level: 4.7

Ellray Jakes the Dragon Slayer! By Sally Warner
A mischievous eight-year-old boy helps his sister with a bully problem, while facing a bully of his own at school.
Reading Level: 4.5

Yolonda's Genius  by  Carol Fenner
Yolanda tries to prove her brother is a musical genius when her family moves out of Chicago to a small town.
Reading Level: 4.8

After Tupac And D Foster by Jacqueline Woodson
In the New York City borough of Queens in 1996, three girls bond over their shared love of Tupac Shakur's music, as together they try to make sense of the unpredictable world in which they live.
Reading Level: 4.7

Fairway Phenom  by Matt  Christopher
Malik has never had any intention of playing golf until he sees a young black golfer being cheered on by a crowd of adoring fans. But where is a kid living in Brooklyn, New York, supposed to play golf?
Grade Level: 4.2

Reading Levels: 5.0 - 5.9

Understanding Photosynthesis With Max Axiom, Super Scientist by Liam O’Donnell
In graphic novel format, follows the adventures of Max Axiom as he explains the science behind photosynthesis.
Reading Level: 5.2

Bud, Not Buddy by Christopher Paul Curtis
Ten-year-old Bud, a motherless boy living in Flint, Michigan during the Great Depression, escapes a bad foster home and sets out in search of the man he believes to be his father--the renowned bandleader, H.E. Calloway of Grand Rapids.
Reading Level: 5.0

Mr. Chickee's Funny Money by Christopher Paul Curtis
Flint Future Detective Club members Steven Carter, his friend Russell, and Russell's huge dog Zoopy solve the mystery of a quadrillion-dollar bill with the image of James Brown on it.
Reading Level: 5.3

All Of The Above by Shelley Pearsall
Five urban middle school students, their teacher, and other community members relate how a school project to build the world's largest tetrahedron affects the lives of everyone involved.
Reading Level: 5.3

Jubilee Journey by Carolyn Meyer
Emily Rose has always felt comfortable growing up in Connecticut with her African-American mother and her "French-American" father, but when they spend some time with her great-grandmother in Texas, Emily Rose learns about her black heritage and uncovers some new and exciting parts of her own identity.
Reading Level: 5.4
   

Mr. Chickee’s Messy Mission by Christopher Paul Curtis
The Flint Future Detective Club members chase a gnome into a mural, only to find the mysterious Mr. Chickee on the other side.
Reading Level: 5.6

Explosive World Of Volcanoes With Max Axiom, Super Scientist
In graphic novel format, follow the adventures of Max Axiom as he explains the science behind volcanoes.
Reading Level: 5.5

Watsons Go To Birmingham, 1963 by Christopher Paul Curtis
The ordinary interactions and everyday routines of the Watsons, an African American family living in Flint, Michigan, are drastically changed after they go to visit Grandma in Alabama in the summer of 1963.
Reading Level: 5.0
